\section{Introduction}

Tabular data remains one of the most common data modalities in applied machine learning, appearing in domains such as materials science, healthcare, e-commerce, and human-resource analytics. Despite the rapid progress of deep learning on images and language, GBDTs such as XGBoost and LightGBM remain difficult to outperform on many tabular tasks~\citep{grinsztajn2022tree,holzmuller2024better}.

This project replicates and evaluates xRFM, a recent feature learning approach for tabular data based on the average gradient outer product (AGOP)~\citep{beaglehole2025xrfm,radhakrishnan2024mechanism}. Instead of relying only on fixed input features, xRFM uses gradient-based information from a learned prediction function to estimate directions in the input space that are most relevant to the target. This produces both a predictive model and an interpretable feature-relevance signal. The central question of our replication is therefore whether AGOP-driven feature learning can offer a competitive alternative to strong tabular baselines, especially when compared with XGBoost, LightGBM, and a multilayer perceptron (MLP).

We evaluate all models under a unified pipeline across five public tabular datasets covering regression and binary classification, large and small sample sizes, and mixed feature types (Diamonds, Superconductivity, Online Shoppers, Stroke, HR Attrition). Our main finding is that tree-based GBDTs lead overall, while xRFM shows selective strength on small-sample and fully numerical tasks. Section~2 describes methodology, Section~3 reports results, and Sections~4--5 discuss findings and conclusions.
