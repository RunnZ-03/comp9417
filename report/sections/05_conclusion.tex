In this work, we replicated the core experiments of \citet{beaglehole2025xrfm}, comparing xRFM
against XGBoost, LightGBM, and MLP on five tabular datasets. Our results broadly confirm the
paper's claims: xRFM is competitive on regression tasks, particularly those with high-dimensional
feature spaces, while GBDTs maintain a clear advantage on datasets with strong axis-aligned
structure. LightGBM emerges as the most training-time efficient model across all settings.

Interpretability analysis confirms that xRFM's AGOP provides a principled and data-driven
measure of feature importance, comparable to permutation importance but arising naturally from
the model rather than requiring post-hoc computation. Scalability experiments show that both
GBDT methods scale near-linearly in training time, while MLP incurs substantially higher costs.

Future work could investigate xRFM on larger datasets ($n > 100{,}000$) where its $O(n \log n)$
training complexity may become a decisive advantage over MLP-based approaches.
